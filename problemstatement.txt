Background
Millions of individuals remain uninsured, underinsured, or overpay for plans that don't match
their actual profile. Finding suitable insurance involves visiting multiple portals, filling repetitive
forms, and decoding complex jargon without personalized guidance. The opportunity is a single
intelligent platform that uses healthcare data and trained models to recommend the right
coverage with clarity and transparency.

Problem Statement
Build a full-stack, AI-assisted insurance recommendation platform tailored for everyday
individuals seeking the right cover. The platform should collect a user's health, demographic,
and financial profile, use trained models to assess health risk and care needs, and recommend
the most suitable insurance plans with explainable, data-driven reasoning — delivered through a
clean, intuitive interface.

SCENARIO
A 35-year-old professional with a family history of diabetes fills in age, income, BMI,
and lifestyle details, then uploads a recent blood sugar report. The platform extracts
key health indicators, feeds them into a trained model, and instantly surfaces the top 5
insurance plans — each with a plain-English explanation: "Recommended because
your HbA1c levels indicate pre-diabetic risk — this plan covers diabetes-related
hospitalization from day one."

Objectives
— Collect a user's health, demographic, and financial profile via a guided multi-step form
— Extract key health indicators from uploaded clinical documents using AI
— Train models on healthcare datasets to understand health risk and care needs
— Generate personalized recommendations aligned to healthcare needs and financial
constraints
— Deliver explainable recommendations so users understand why a plan was suggested
— Allow side-by-side plan comparison and filtering
— Implement secure JWT-based authentication with privacy controls
— Deliver a responsive UI across desktop and mobile

What to Build
Data & Feature Layer
Structured ingestion of health, demographic, and financial data through a guided multi-step
intake form, enriched with AI extraction from uploaded clinical documents (lab reports,
prescriptions, diagnostic PDFs).

Model Layer
Train models using publicly available healthcare datasets to understand health risk profiles and

care needs. Recommended approaches: classification models, scoring systems, and similarity-
based recommendation engines.

Recommendation Engine
Consumes the trained model's outputs to generate personalized insurance plan
recommendations:
· Suitability score
· Plain-English explanation of why the plan was suggested
· Coverage highlights
· Premium breakdown

Plan Explorer
A filterable insurance plan listing with price range, coverage type, and insurer filters. Each plan
has a dedicated detail page with coverage highlights, premium breakdown, benefits, exclusions,
and pros/cons.

Dashboard
· Recommended plans with suitability scores and explanations
· Side-by-side plan comparison (up to 3 plans)
· User risk profile summary generated by the model

Feature Expectations
Must-Have
— Secure user registration and JWT-based login
— Multi-step profile intake form collecting health, demographic, and financial data
— AI extraction of key health values from uploaded clinical documents
— Trained model for health risk assessment and plan suitability scoring
— Explainable recommendations with plain-English reasoning
— Plan listing with filters and side-by-side comparison
— Privacy controls for sensitive health and financial data
— Responsive UI across desktop and mobile

Good to Have (optional)
— Chatbot guiding users through profile intake
— Plan affordability simulator — "what if my income changes?"
— Multi-language support including at least one regional Indian language
— Model confidence indicator showing certainty of each recommendation

Dataset Guidance
Two categories of datasets are required for this idea.
— Healthcare training datasets: UCI Heart Disease, Kaggle diabetes datasets, or AI-generated
synthetic health records
— Insurance plan catalogue: at least 15–20 plans across health, life, and critical illness
categories
— Catalogue should include premium ranges, coverage limits, eligibility criteria, and key
exclusions
